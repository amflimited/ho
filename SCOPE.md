# Hoosier Online — Scope (North Star)

> The fixed target. Every change is measured against this document.
> If a thing does not serve **Side A**, **Side B**, or **the spine**, it does not get built.

_Last set: 2026-06-12_

---

## The hook

Deliver a **personalized service to single-operator small businesses**, produced
**mostly autonomously** so it **scales**. Indiana first, then wider.

The richness of the personalization — built from what we know about the operator,
their business, and the choices they made — is the entire competitive advantage.
The pitch is so specific to one person that it converts. That richness is the weapon.

We offer the right choice for the right person: a one-time build and an ongoing
managed presence both live under the same personalization engine.

---

## The shape: two systems and one spine

```
   Side B  →  [ ONE business record ]  →  Side A
 (research &     the shared spine        (the pitch:
  data mgmt)   — context in, pitch out —  outreach + /go page)
              ↑  operator decides  ↑
                  who / when
```

### The spine — one canonical business record
- A single source of truth for "a business." Side B writes it; Side A reads it.
- The schema **is** the contract between the two sides. Define it once, change it deliberately.
- This is the thing that never existed. Its absence is why the same business has been
  represented across ~10 competing model files, why functions collide, and why every
  past version re-did the same work. Fixing this is the foundation.

### Side B — Research & Data Platform (operator-facing)
- Take a business → produce a **deep, clean, structured record**.
- Optimized for: depth of context, and data we can trust.
- The most effective research and data-management surface we can build. Nothing else.

### Side A — The Pitch (customer-facing)
- Read **one record** → generate one personalized pitch delivered in two coordinated pieces:
  1. a **masterful outreach message** that hooks the operator, and
  2. a **flawless, conversion-built `/go` page** it lands on.
- The message and the page share one context and one voice; they complement each other
  by design. The email earns the click; the page closes.
- Optimized for one thing: **conversion**.

### The operator (you)
- Choose who to contact, when, and which campaigns to try. By hand, on purpose.
- No software is built to manage piles, route work, or track a pipeline. That is a
  human decision, kept human deliberately.

---

## Explicitly out of scope (delete on sight)

These exist only to track/route manual work. The operator is the controller, so the
software does not need to be:

- Command Center / portal dashboard as a work-routing hub
- Market Map / market-coverage tracking
- Production Lane (home / check / plan)
- State Audit / command-center audit
- Triage queues and pile management
- Send trays / send tracking as a subsystem
- The overlapping, competing `*-model.php` files, once folded into the one spine

---

## The test for any future change

Before building anything, it must answer **yes** to one of these:

1. Does it define or strengthen **the one business record** (the spine)?
2. Does it make **Side B** gather richer context or manage data more cleanly?
3. Does it make **Side A** convert better — a sharper outreach message or a better `/go` page?

If it's "no" to all three, it is not built. If it's "it helps me track or route work,"
it is not built — that's the operator's job, not the software's.

## Why this document exists

Past sessions re-guessed the target every time, so they kept rebuilding the wrong axis:
manual pile-tracking tooling, when the actual hook is autonomous personalized delivery
at scale. This is the fixed target so that stops. Measure against it; cut against it.
